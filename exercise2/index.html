How to Make a Planting Pot



Many gardeners get an early start on planting seeds by starting them in planting pots indoors and transplanting them later to the garden when the danger of frost is past. Commercial pots made from compressed peat moss are available but it is easy to make your own.






1 . . .



Take a sheet of newspaper and cut into six inches strips about 30 inches (76.2 cm) long.

2 . . .



Get an aluminum soda can and wrap the strip around the can leaving about two inches extending beyond the bottom of the can. As you wrap the paper continuously around the can, tuck the excess paper to cover the bottom of the can and create a nearly flat base for the pot.

3 . . .



Secure the strip of paper with tape, especially across the bottom.

4 . . .



Set upright in a waterproof tray where they will stay until ready for transplanting.

5 . . .



Fill with seed starting mix or regular potting mix, leaving one-half inch at the top.

6 . . .



Plant two or three seeds in each pot according to the directions on the seed package and water.

7 . . .



Know that when it is ready for transplanting,to take the tray to the garden site and carefully place the pot in the transplant hole.

8 . . .



Poke several holes around the bottom of the planting pot to encourage root growth. Place the pot it in the hole and fill in with soil. The newspaper will soon rot.

9 . . .



Finished.
